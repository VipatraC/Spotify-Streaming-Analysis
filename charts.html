Spotify Streaming Analysis

This project analyzes Spotify track data to uncover the key factors that influence song popularity. By exploring audio features such as energy, danceability, tempo, and genre, the analysis aims to identify patterns that contribute to streaming success and provide data-driven insights relevant to the music industry.

Chart 01

Distribution of Songs by Genre

Breaks down the dataset composition by genre. Confirms that the sample is broadly balanced across EDM, Rap, Pop, R&B, Latin, and Rock — ensuring genre-level findings are not skewed by unequal representation.

Chart 02

Track Popularity Distribution by Genre

Displays how popularity scores are distributed across six genres. Reveals whether certain genres (e.g. pop, R&B) consistently produce high-performing tracks, or if popularity varies widely within a genre.

Chart 03

Number of Songs & Average Popularity by Year

Tracks the growth of Spotify's catalog alongside average track popularity over time. Helps identify which release years produced the most-streamed content and whether catalog size correlates with listener engagement.

Chart 04

Audio Features by Popularity — High vs. Low

Compares the standardized audio profile of high- vs. low-popularity tracks across features like danceability, energy, and instrumentalness. Reveals which sonic characteristics most distinguish a hit from an underperformer.

Chart 05

Correlation Heatmap of Audio Features

Shows pairwise correlations between all audio features. Highlights which features move together (e.g. energy and loudness) and which are inversely related (e.g. acousticness and energy), helping to avoid redundant variables in predictive modeling.
