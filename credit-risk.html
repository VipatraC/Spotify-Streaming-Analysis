Credit Risk Modeling
& Simulation

This project builds a credit rating model inspired by how Moody's and S&P assess company risk. Using 3 years of financial statement data (2015–2017), it scores companies across 5 key metrics and assigns ratings from A to CCC — then uses Monte Carlo simulation to estimate how likely each rating group is to default. It's the kind of model banks use to decide who to lend to and at what risk.

Methodology

Corporate Credit Rating Model — Pipeline

1

Historical data collection (2015–2017)

Pulled 3 years of financial statements to avoid look-ahead bias. Ratings are assigned at the start of 2018 using only prior data — mirroring how real rating agencies operate with lagged information.

2

5-pillar financial metric calculation

Computed ROA, Leverage, Current Ratio, Interest Coverage, and Asset Turnover — one metric per risk dimension. Each pillar captures a distinct aspect of a firm's financial health, from profitability to debt burden.

3

Z-score standardization & 3-year averaging

Standardized each metric cross-sectionally so all pillars are on the same scale, then averaged each company's scores over the 3 years to reduce single-year noise and smooth out temporary volatility spikes.

4

Weighted aggregation into a composite score

Applied industry-inspired weights — Leverage 25%, Profitability 20%, Liquidity 20%, Coverage 20%, Efficiency 15% — to produce a single composite risk score per firm. Leverage carries the highest weight as it is most directly tied to default probability.

5

Threshold-based rating assignment

Mapped composite scores to five categories — A, BBB, BB, B, CCC — using z-score cutoffs calibrated to approximate Moody's-style distribution. Monte Carlo simulation is then applied to each rating bucket to estimate default probabilities.
